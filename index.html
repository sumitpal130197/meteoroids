Meteoroids

Meteoroids are small fragments from comets or asteroids, first identified as part of celestial debris in the 19th century. While not attributed to a single discoverer, meteoroids became widely studied after their fiery transformations into meteors. They're observed across Earth's skies during meteor showers like the Perseids.

A meteoroid is a small rocky or metallic object orbiting the Sun. It is essentially space debris, ranging in size from microscopic dust particles to small asteroids. These objects are remnants of comets, asteroids, or other celestial bodies. Most meteoroids never come close to Earth, but some do enter our planet's atmosphere.

Meteorites

Meteorite is a space rock that survives its trip through Earth's atmosphere and lands on the ground. Meteorites come from asteroids, which are rocky objects that orbit the Sun, mostly in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter. Asteroids can collide with each other, breaking apart and creating smaller pieces.

It is a meteoroid that survives its fiery journey through the atmosphere and lands on Earth's surface. These celestial visitors can range in size from tiny pebbles to massive boulders. Meteorites provide valuable insights into the formation and early history of our solar system. Scientists study meteorites to learn about the composition of other celestial bodies and the processes that shaped them.

ASTEROIDS

Asteroids, remnants of the solar system's formation, orbit the sun in diverse paths. Some reside in the main asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter, while others venture closer to Earth's orbit. These rocky bodies range in size from tiny pebbles to massive objects, each with its own unique composition and history.

As silent witnesses to the cosmos's evolution, asteroids offer valuable insights into the early solar system. They also pose a potential threat to Earth, with some near-Earth objects capable of causing significant damage. Asteroids have played a critical role in Earth's geological and biological history. Large asteroid impacts are believed to have caused mass extinction events, such as the one that wiped out the dinosaurs 66 million years ago.

FAQ's

1. How do I know that your meteorites are genuine?

+

All of our meteorites have gone through extensive scientific classification - a process in which dedicated scientists in the field of Meteoritics study a sample of the meteorite using several different techniques including spectroscopy. The scientist first confirms what type of meteorite they are looking at, followed by an in-depth study to determine its mineral compositions and origins. ﻿This is a very general summary of the very long, tedious and expensive process that scientists take to confirm not only the type, but the parent body it once broke away from.

2. How do you know for sure that a meteorite is from our Moon/Mars?

+

We appreciate this question no matter how many times it is asked. The simple answer is "science". Both our Moon and planet Mars lack an atmosphere. When asteroids strike these celestial objects (which happens quite frequently - think of the Moon with all of those craters!), fragments eject from the impact and occasionally make their way into Earth’s orbit. If the stone survives its cataclysmic entry into Earth, it lands on the ground and becomes known as a meteorite.

3. Where are your meteorites found?

+

The short answer is - EVERYWHERE! We have Meteorites from just about every Continent. We even have Meteorites that have fallen through houses, cars and even a cow!

4. Will I receive a case with my purchase?

+

90% of our orders will ship with a display case. The percentage that does not is because your Meteorite may not fit in a case or be safe to ship in for your particular order. Instead, you may find that your order arrives with your new Meteorite wrapped separately in cardboard from the display case (or, as mentioned, sometimes without a case) - this is intentional. This prevents the stone from shifting around in the case during transit and possibly cracking. We choose to avoid that possibility.

5. Will I receive a Certificate of Authenticity with my purchase?

+

Yes - all meteorites come with a signed Certificate of Authenticity.

6. How do I care for my meteorites?

+

With iron/stony-iron meteorites - you'll need to pay a little more attention to them than you would a stony meteorite. It's always a battle of humidity vs meteorite, and usually depends on where you live that will determine the level of extra attention that you'll need to give it. The higher the humidity, the bigger hit the meteorite will take. But with good habits, care and attention to your meteorites, you'll catch any issues before they ever become a serious problem.
